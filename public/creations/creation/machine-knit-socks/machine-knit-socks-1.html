Machine-Knit Socks

These are socks I knitted on my Singer Mod 700 knitting machine. It's adapted from this pattern. Although it's a bit expensive, and I'm not sure if $30 is worth it for this style of pattern book, but I paid it so ig it is lol. This photo shows the socks as they've just come from the sink from soaking. They're a bit wet and wrinkly at the moment, but I was super excited to share them, haha. I'll update the photos in the future. I've definitely learned a LOT from this project, and I can't wait to make more socks!

  • Pattern: The ULTIMATE Machine Knit Socks (eBook)
  • Yarn: CoBaSi 57 & 69

Overall, there's some good and bad things about the pattern I used.

Pros

  • Quickly Knit Up
  • Don't need to fiddle the with ribber too much
  • very versatile pattern- I made an excel sheet I can input measurements in and it's super customizable

Cons

  • SEAMING
    • I absolutely HATE seaming my knit items. It keeps me from completing projects that are basically finished. These socks require minimal seaming as the sew-as-you-go method closes the sole of the foot while knitting the top of the foot. However, even the small amount I had to seam here took forever, haha.

go back home?
